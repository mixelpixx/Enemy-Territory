> **For agentic workers:** REQUIRED SUB-SKILL: Use superpowers:subagent-driven-development (recommended) or superpowers:executing-plans to implement this plan task-by-task. Steps use checkbox (`- [ ]`) syntax for tracking.

**Goal:** Every modern renderer2 feature — HDR/tonemap/bloom, shadow mapping (incl. soft shadows and sun cascades), per-pixel dynamic lights, normal/specular/parallax materials, water/reflection, plus the bonus post-effects (SSAO, depth of field, camera post-FX) — works when switched ON under `cl_renderer gl2`, each behind its own cvar, all default-OFF, with the all-off state verified pixel-equivalent to the accepted R2-4 baseline.

**Architecture:** Research verified (2026-06-12): all features are compiled into the DLL already; every modern cvar defaults OFF upstream and nothing in our scripts/bridge/cmake overrides them — so the accepted R2-4 baseline IS the all-off state, and this increment is bring-up of the ON states. Per-feature kill-switches are the descope mechanism: a feature that proves broken is documented as known-off and the increment still ships. One structural risk to clear first: the bridge cvar proxy narrows their 64-bit cvar flags to our int via `BRDG_CVAR_FLAGMASK` (bits 0-13, tr2_bridge_theirs.c) — their `CVAR_ARCHIVE_ND` and `CVAR_LATCH` bit values must be confirmed to survive the narrowing, or latched/archived behavior of every modern cvar silently breaks (Task 1).
